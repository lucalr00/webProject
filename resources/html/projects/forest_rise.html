ForestRise

Introduction

Where

Our project targets medium-large forests that are or have been subject to deforestation, fires or infestations. ZeroCarbon's experts are currently deployed in the Amazon forest, black forest, Congo forest and boreal forest.

When

After choosing the right place for the project, our company does not stop until the complete restoration of the forest and its fauna. The duration therefore depends on the size of the area to be reforested and the problem that affected it.

Objectives

Restore the forest

Through the project, we aim for the complete restoration of destroyed or ruined areas. The objective is to re-establish green areas to reduce CO2 emissions and repopulate the wooded areas with local fauna from which habitat has been taken away. At the moment this project has found a 90% effectiveness in reforestation, restoring wooded areas without further polluting them

Reuse of forest material

Obviously reforestation requires (also based on the type of damage that the forest has suffered) first a complete cleaning of the undergrowth before starting repopulation. This involves a large amount of organic material that must be disposed of or reused in some way. Our company aims for a complete disposal of waste materials so that they can be reused for the production of fertilizer for the new forest, or used in other sustainable ZeroCarbon projects. See also project FoRestore

Methods

Research and Development

Through revenues from our other projects and thanks to donations from our funders, we are able to fund research to maximize the effectiveness of forest restoration. It also funds research to prevent and treat plant diseases that can spread and cause epidemics, addressing the root cause of the problem
